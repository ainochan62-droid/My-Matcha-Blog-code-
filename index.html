Matcha

First of all, I want to explain the difference between matcha and green tea because people often confuse matcha with green tea. However, this is understandable because matcha and green tea actually have many similarities, for instance their taste, color, and scent.

Matcha is one of the kinds of green tea and has a stronger caffeine content and aroma than regular green tea. The tea leaves are finely ground before brewing. Moreover, the entire tea leaf is consumed, which is different from drinking green tea.

Green tea must be strained before consumption and has a milder flavor and aroma. Its caffeine content is not as strong as matcha's.

Kinds of Matcha

Matcha originated in Japan. Its differences depend on the regions and cities where it is produced.

Famous Matcha in Japan

Matcha Origin(City) Infomation
Uji(宇治) Kyoto(京都) Famous for high-quality and ceremonial-grade matcha with deep umami and low bitterness.
Nishio(西尾) Aichi(愛知) Known for creamy texture, sweetness, and aroma, great for lattes and culinary uses.
Yame(八女) Fukuoka(福岡) Famous for deep, smooth umami flavor, natural buttery sweetness, and low bitterness.
